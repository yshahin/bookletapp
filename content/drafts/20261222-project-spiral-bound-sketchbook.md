A custom spiral-bound sketchbook lets you choose the perfect paper, size, and cover for your drawing practice.

**TL;DR**
- Punch evenly spaced holes through covers and pages with a hole punch or drill
- Wire coil or plastic spiral threads through the holes for 360-degree page rotation
- Custom paper selection means you can optimize for your specific medium

![Hero: DIY Spiral Bound Sketchbook Project](/images/hero/project-spiral-bound-sketchbook.jpg)

## Project Overview

**Finished size:** 8.5" x 11" (Letter size) or A4
**Page count:** 50 sheets (100 pages)
**Binding:** Metal spiral coil, 5:1 pitch
**Time required:** 1-2 hours
**Skill level:** Beginner to Intermediate

## Materials List

| Material | Quantity | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Drawing paper | 50 sheets | 70-100 lb (100-150 gsm) |
| Chipboard | 2 pieces | Same size as paper, or cut to size |
| Metal coil | 1 | 12mm diameter for 50 sheets |
| Coil crimping pliers | 1 | Or needle-nose pliers |
| Spiral binding machine | 1 | With 5:1 pitch |
| Decorative paper | Optional | For covering chipboard |
| PVA glue | If covering | Adhesive for covers |


## Understanding Spiral Binding Machines

Before starting, familiarize yourself with your binding equipment:

#### Punch Section

- Adjustable paper guide
- Die that creates round holes
- Capacity indicator (sheets per punch)
- Margin depth adjustment

#### Coil Inserter (if included)

- Motor or manual crank
- Spins coil through punched holes
- Guides keep coil aligned

**Note:** Many spiral machines are punch-only. You can insert coils by hand if needed.

## Step 1: Prepare the Paper

#### Selecting Drawing Paper

Choose paper appropriate for your medium:

| Medium | Paper Weight | Surface |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Pencil | 70-80 lb | Smooth to medium |
| Charcoal | 80-90 lb | Medium tooth |
| Marker | 70-80 lb | Smooth |
| Mixed media | 90-100 lb | Medium |
| Light wash | 90-100 lb | Cold press |

#### Cutting to Size

If your paper isn't pre-cut:

1. Measure and mark precisely
2. Use a rotary cutter and straight edge
3. Cut multiple sheets together for efficiency
4. Check that all sheets are identical size

#### Checking Grain Direction

- Grain should run parallel to the binding edge
- Test by bending: paper bends more easily with the grain
- Pages will turn more smoothly with correct grain

## Step 2: Prepare the Covers

Chipboard covers protect your sketchbook and provide rigidity.

#### Cutting Chipboard

1. Measure to match paper size exactly
2. Use a utility knife and metal ruler
3. Make multiple passes rather than forcing through
4. Sand any rough edges

#### Optional: Covering the Chipboard

For a finished appearance:

**Front cover:**
1. Cut decorative paper 1" larger than board on all sides
2. Apply PVA to board
3. Position paper, smooth out bubbles
4. Turn edges, mitering corners
5. Apply endsheet inside if desired

**Back cover:**
1. Can match front or use simpler material
2. Follow same covering process


## Step 3: Punch the Holes

#### Machine Setup

1. Adjust margin depth (typically 3/8" to 1/2")
2. Check that dies are aligned
3. Insert side guide to match paper width
4. Test punch on scrap paper

#### Punching Process

1. **Stack neatly:** Align all pages and covers
2. **Check capacity:** Don't exceed machine's per-punch limit (usually 10-15 sheets)
3. **Align against guide:** Ensure consistent hole placement
4. **Punch firmly:** Complete stroke for clean holes
5. **Continue in batches:** Punch all sheets
6. **Check alignment:** Stack punched sheets to verify holes line up

**Trouble spots:**
- First and last sheets most visible - punch carefully
- Chipboard may require separate punching
- Clear any paper chad from dies between batches


## Step 4: Select and Size the Coil

#### Choosing Coil Diameter

The coil must accommodate all your sheets plus covers without being too loose:

| Total Thickness | Coil Diameter |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Up to 6mm | 8mm coil |
| 6-10mm | 10mm coil |
| 10-15mm | 12mm coil |
| 15-20mm | 14mm coil |

**Measuring thickness:**
1. Stack all punched materials
2. Press together firmly
3. Measure with ruler
4. Choose coil one size up from measurement

#### Cutting Coil to Length

1. Unroll coil from spool
2. Measure against punched stack
3. Cut so coil extends 1/2 loop beyond each end
4. Cut through loop at an angle (easier insertion)
5. Use wire cutters designed for coil

## Step 5: Insert the Coil

#### Hand Insertion Method

If you don't have a coil inserter:

1. Hold the stack with holes facing you
2. Start coil at top or bottom
3. Begin threading through first hole
4. Rotate coil clockwise while pushing forward
5. Thread enters next hole automatically
6. Continue until coil emerges at far end
7. Adjust so equal coil extends at each end

**Tips:**
- Keep pages loosely fanned to see holes
- Don't force - if stuck, back up and realign
- Patience is key for first attempts

#### Machine Insertion

If you have an electric inserter:

1. Load coil into spindle
2. Align punched stack in guide
3. Engage motor
4. Coil spins through automatically
5. Cut coil when fully inserted


## Step 6: Crimp the Coil Ends

Crimping prevents the coil from unscrewing out of the holes.

#### Using Coil Crimping Pliers

1. Position pliers on coil loop beyond last hole
2. Squeeze to flatten loop
3. Bend flattened loop inward, toward the pages
4. Repeat on other end

#### Using Needle-Nose Pliers

1. Grip final coil loop
2. Squeeze to flatten
3. Bend inward with fingers or pliers
4. Creates functional but less refined crimp

**Quality check:** Tug gently on covers - coil should not pull through.

## Step 7: Final Inspection and Finishing

#### Check Your Work

- [ ] All pages turn freely
- [ ] No pages catching on coil
- [ ] Covers sit flat
- [ ] Coil ends securely crimped
- [ ] No sharp wire ends exposed

#### Optional Additions

**Corner rounding:**
- Use corner rounder on pages and covers
- Prevents dog-ears
- More professional appearance

**Tab dividers:**
- Add pre-punched tabs to mark sections
- Useful for organizing different subjects

**Elastic closure:**
- Attach elastic band to hold book closed
- Thread through coil at one end
- Wrap around fore edge


## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Holes don't align | Inconsistent punching | Repunch misaligned sheets |
| Coil too loose | Wrong size selected | Use smaller diameter coil |
| Coil too tight | Wrong size or thick paper | Use larger diameter coil |
| Pages catching | Bent coil | Straighten coil or replace |
| Coil unscrewing | Poor crimping | Re-crimp ends more securely |
| Covers warping | Humidity or uneven covering | Press under weight overnight |

## Variations

#### Mini Sketchbook

- 5.5" x 8.5" (half letter)
- 30 sheets
- Pocket-sized for travel
- Use smaller coil diameter

#### Watercolor Sketchbook

- 140 lb cold press paper
- Fewer sheets (20-30) due to thickness
- Larger coil needed
- Consider waterproof cover

#### Mixed Media Journal

- Variety of papers (drawing, collage, watercolor)
- Heavier chipboard covers
- Extra-large coil
- Reinforced corners
